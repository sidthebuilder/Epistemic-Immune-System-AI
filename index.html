Executive Summary

Enterprise AI systems are failing silently—not through catastrophic collapse, but through epistemic rot. Binding decisions fall stale without anyone noticing. Unresolved questions drop below retrieval thresholds and become invisible. Speculative hypotheses are cited as settled fact, their untested status laundered through repetition. Furthermore, modern enterprises operate in siloed departments (e.g., Legal, Engineering), creating cross-departmental epistemic conflicts that cannot be resolved due to strict data sovereignty constraints. Meanwhile, the emerging infrastructure layer promised to solve these problems—context graphs—captures decision traces and governance metadata but remains blind to the epistemic status of the knowledge it stores and the “unknown-unknowns” missing from its topology.

This paper introduces a comprehensive framework designed to operationalize organizational truth. First, we introduce the Epistemic Immune System (EIS)—a self-monitoring layer that sits atop epistemic context graphs and continuously scans for epistemic degradation: status creep, staleness, ignorance collapse, adversarial anomalies, and Epistemic Dark Matter (topological structural voids). Second, we complement the immune system with Domain Velocity Epistemic Decay (DVED), an extension of existing epistemic decay architectures that modulates forgetting rates by domain-specific velocity, correcting a fundamental misspecification in class-specific-only decay.

Third, we introduce a completely novel architectural breakthrough: Zero-Knowledge Epistemic Cascades (ZKEC). This mechanism utilizes Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) to automatically identify and decay conflicting projects across isolated departmental ledgers without exposing the underlying proprietary text. Finally, we integrate these contributions into the Epistemic Context Graph (ECG), a structured representation where every decision trace node carries OIDA-style epistemic classification, a domain-velocity modulated importance score, and a continuous health vector.

The composite architecture is the first proposal to unify domain-aware temporal dynamics, continuous epistemic health monitoring, and privacy-preserving cryptographic conflict resolution into a single enterprise AI infrastructure. We provide a formal specification of each component, a reference implementation architecture, a taxonomy of detection signatures for epistemic pathogens, and a research agenda for empirical validation.

Section 1

Introduction

The Silent Crisis in Enterprise Knowledge

Enterprise AI deployments exhibit a well-documented paradox: investment continues to accelerate—the market is projected at $7–$12 billion in 2026, with knowledge-graph infrastructure growing at nearly $25–$100 billion (Korra.ai, 2025).

Prevailing diagnoses focus on retrieval fidelity: better embeddings, denser indexes, hybrid search, and graph-structured retrieval. These are legitimate improvements within their scope but share a structural blind spot. They operate on an epistemically flat substrate, treating all retrieved content as equally authoritative regardless of whether it records a binding board decision, an abandoned brainstorming memo, a contested claim, or an unresolved inquiry. The result is what Kumar (2026a) terms the coherence trap: AI systems produce outputs that are syntactically fluent and semantically grounded yet epistemically incoherent.

Four Emerging Problems

Recent research has surfaced four degradation vectors that compound the coherence trap and remain unaddressed by any unified framework:

  1. Uniform epistemic decay is misspecified. Bottino et al. (2026) propose class-specific decay in their OIDA architecture. However, Karhade (2026) demonstrates that even class-specific decay is too coarse: different knowledge types exhibit fundamentally different temporal dynamics depending on domain velocity. A Decision in a product roadmap domain changes meaning faster than a Decision in regulatory compliance.
  2. Organizational knowledge degrades through multiple silent vectors. Gerlach & Lange (2026) describe the “AI knowledge trap”: a self-reinforcing cycle where AI takes over tasks, human expertise erodes, AI models become outdated, and the expertise needed to update them is no longer available. Multi-agent systems exhibit “problem drift” (Kaesberg et al., 2026).
  3. Emerging context graph infrastructure lacks epistemic status. Gartner defines context graphs as evolving structures that connect data, states, actions, and goals into a single graph. Context graphs capture decision traces but do not capture epistemic status. Without epistemic annotation, context graphs give agents the history of decisions without the authority of those decisions.
  4. Cross-Departmental Data Silos and Unknown-Unknowns. Large organizations rely on highly siloed local ledgers (e.g., Legal vs. Engineering). Currently, when engineering makes an assumption that violates a hidden legal patent, the enterprise AI cannot identify the conflict without breaching data sovereignty. Furthermore, current systems only model what is known or explicitly asked, ignoring the vast “unknown-unknowns” (Epistemic Dark Matter) hiding within the graph topology.

This Paper’s Contribution

We propose a unified architecture that addresses all four problems simultaneously:

  • Domain Velocity Epistemic Decay (DVED): An extension of OIDA-style decay modulated by domain velocity coefficients.
  • The Epistemic Immune System (EIS): A continuous monitoring layer detecting Status Creep, Staleness, Ignorance Collapse, Adversarial Anomalies, and Epistemic Dark Matter.
  • Zero-Knowledge Epistemic Cascades (ZKEC): Our proprietary cryptographic routing mechanism for resolving multi-departmental truth without exposing raw text.
  • The Epistemic Context Graph (ECG): The foundational graph data structure unifying the aforementioned systems.
Section 2

Converging Threads

The Epistemic Immune System, Domain Velocity Epistemic Decay, and Epistemic Context Graph emerge from distinct, well-documented threads of recent research that have not yet been unified.

2.1 The Epistemic Infrastructure Thread

Bottino et al. (2026) provide the foundational OIDA architecture. Kumar (2026a) extends OIDA with the Federated Epistemic Ledger (FEL) and Workflow-Based Epistemic Decay.

2.2 The Adaptive Decay Thread

Karhade (2026) provides the critical insight that uniform decay is misspecified, proposing orthogonal signals of velocity and volatility. Empirical results show uniform decay performs 18× worse than no temporal weighting at all.

2.3 The Silent Degradation Thread

Multiple lines of research (Gerlach & Lange, 2026; Kaesberg et al., 2026; Levin, 2026) document that organizational knowledge degrades quietly and invisibly through mechanisms that are cumulative and undetected by standard monitoring.

2.4 The Context Graph Thread

Gartner (2026) positioned context graphs as foundational infrastructure for agentic AI, predicting that over 50% of AI agent systems will rely on them by 2028. Context graphs provide the “what”, “who”, “how”, and “why” of organizational decisions.

2.5 The Digital Immunology Thread

Practitioner forums (Cybernative.AI, 2025) propose “epistemic immune systems” metaphorically, with no formal architecture ever specified.

2.6 The Gap: Cross-Departmental Sovereignty and ZKPs

No existing framework successfully integrates these systems while preserving local data privacy. Large enterprises cannot federate their data into a single giant vector database. While Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) have been widely adopted in cryptocurrency and decentralized identity, their application to cross-departmental epistemic conflict resolution represents a massive, untapped frontier. This paper proposes the first proprietary synthesis of ZKPs with Epistemic Ledgers to achieve organizational truth without compromising sovereignty.

Section 3

The Proposed Architecture

We now formally specify the integrated components.

3.1 Domain Velocity Epistemic Decay (DVED)

OIDA defines Knowledge Objects (KOs) with epistemic class C and class-specific decay function δ(C, t). We introduce a domain velocity coefficient vd ∈ [0,1] for each domain d. The effective decay function becomes:

δ_effective(C, d, t) = δ(C, t) · (1 − α · vd)

Velocity Estimation:

v_d = |{supersession events in domain d}| / |{total KOs in domain d}| · |{time window}|¹

Fast-moving domains (high vd) accelerate decay; slow-moving domains (low vd) suppress it.

Velocity Tier Example Domains Typical Half-Life vd
Hypersonic Product roadmaps, competitive intelligence, LLM releases 7–14 days ≈ 0.9
Standard Market analysis, team structures, project plans 30–90 days ≈ 0.5
Frozen Regulatory requirements, legal precedent, HTTP specs 1–5 years ≈ 0.1

3.2 The Epistemic Immune System (EIS)

The EIS maintains, for each Knowledge Object, a health vector:

H(KO) = [h_status, h_staleness, h_ignorance, h_adversarial, h_darkmatter]

where each component ∈ [0,1] (0 = healthy, 1 = critically degraded). We define five categories of epistemic pathogens:

Category 1: Status Creep

Definition: A Knowledge Object is being cited as if it carries higher epistemic authority than its assigned class warrants.

Detection Signature: For a Hypothesis H, monitor the ratio R = |citations of H as Evidence| / |total citations of H|. If R > 0.3 over a trailing 30-day window, flag for review.

Example: A product manager’s speculative projection (“Feature X will boost engagement by 20%”) is cited in three subsequent documents as established fact, with no validation experiment ever conducted.

Category 2: Staleness

Definition: A Knowledge Object with high epistemic authority (Decision, Evidence) has received no reinforcement signals beyond its expected refresh interval.

Detection Signature: If t_last_reinforcement > θD · (1 + vd), flag for review. (Baseline θD = 90 days, adjusted by domain velocity.)

Example: A board decision from 8 months ago remains “binding” in the ledger, but the market conditions on which it was based have shifted dramatically.

Category 3: Ignorance Collapse

Definition: An unresolved Question has decayed below retrieval threshold—the organization has “forgotten that it doesn’t know” something important.

Detection Signature: If I(Q) < Imin and t_open(Q) > 30 days, the question is both old and invisible.

Example: An unanswered regulatory question about patent clearance, opened 6 weeks ago, has decayed below retrieval threshold. Agents no longer surface it. The organization has effectively “forgotten” a critical risk.

Category 4: Adversarial Anomalies

Definition: Sudden reclassification of Knowledge Objects without corresponding governance activity, indicative of malicious insider manipulation or autonomous agent hallucination.

Detection Signature: If V_transition > μ + 3σ within a 24-hour window, trigger immediate alert and quarantine affected KOs.

Additional Check: Transitions from Hypothesis to Decision without explicit human sign-off are always flagged, regardless of velocity.

Category 5: Epistemic Dark Matter (Structural Voids)

Definition: By applying topological network analysis, the EIS detects “Structural Voids”—areas where knowledge should exist given the density of surrounding concepts, but is missing.

Detection Signature: High eigenvector centrality nodes lacking standard bi-directional validation edges are flagged as structural voids.

Health Impact: h_darkmatter flags the surrounding sub-graph as “highly volatile / unknown,” prompting the organization to investigate the gap before it becomes a critical failure point.

Alert Level H_aggregate Response
Green < 0.3 No action
Yellow 0.3 – 0.7 Flag in dashboard; no interruption to retrieval
Orange 0.7 – 0.9 Suppress importance score; notify governance board
Red > 0.9 Quarantine affected KOs; require human review before reactivation

3.3 Zero-Knowledge Epistemic Cascades (ZKEC)

To resolve structural voids and contradictions across siloed departments, we introduce our completely novel architectural mechanism: Zero-Knowledge Epistemic Cascades.

The Mechanism

When the Engineering agent records a Decision (e.g., “Implement Feature X”), it generates a cryptographic proof of its epistemic state. The global Epistemic Router checks this proof against the Legal department’s private ledger.

The Cascade

If the router detects an Epistemic Contradiction (e.g., the feature violates a confidential patent), it triggers a cascade. It instantly drops the importance score of all downstream Engineering tasks relying on Feature X, alerting the project manager to a “Cross-Departmental Void.”

The Privacy Guarantee

This is achieved without ever revealing Legal’s actual patent data to the Engineering team’s LLM. It is a purely mathematical proof of contradiction, implemented using zk-SNARK protocols at the graph-edge level.

3.4 The Epistemic Context Graph (ECG)

We extend Gartner’s context graph with three epistemic dimensions at every decision trace node:

  1. Epistemic Class (EC): OIDA-style classification: Decision, Plan, Evidence, Hypothesis, Observation, Question, Constraint, Narrative, Evaluation.
  2. Domain-Velocity Modulated Importance Score (IDVED): A dynamic score maintained by the DVED layer.
  3. Health Vector (H): A continuous health assessment maintained by the EIS layer.

Section 4

Reference Implementation Architecture

We provide a reference implementation as a set of interoperating services.

  • Service 1: Velocity Estimator. A Python service that periodically (daily) computes domain velocity vd from the ECG’s supersession log. Output is written to a velocity registry consumed by the DVED modulator.
  • Service 2: DVED Modulator. A middleware layer that intercepts importance score updates and applies the domain velocity correction δ_effective(C, d, t) = δ(C, t) · (1 − α · vd). Configurable α parameter.
  • Service 3: EIS Scanner. A scheduled service (configurable interval; suggested: 4-hourly) that scans the ECG for all five pathogen categories. Maintains an in-memory immune memory store for pathogen signatures.
  • Service 4: ZKP Router. Handles the Zero-Knowledge Epistemic Cascades using zk-SNARK protocols for fast validation across departmental boundary APIs. Produces alert events when Epistemic Contradictions are detected.
  • Service 5: ECG Enrichment API. A REST endpoint that accepts standard context graph nodes and returns enriched nodes with EC, IDVED, and H fields populated.

Technology Stack (Reference): Python (FastAPI for services), PostgreSQL (ECG storage, velocity registry, immune memory), Redis (real-time health vectors, caching), Docker Compose (deployment).

Latency Targets: DVED modulation: sub-10ms per KO; EIS full scan (10&sup6; KOs): sub-60 seconds; ECG enrichment API: sub-100ms per query.

Section 5

Evaluation Methodology

We propose a staged evaluation approach:

  1. Stage 1: Offline Validation of DVED. Using the organizational epistemic stress corpora released by Bottino et al. (2026), compare retrieval performance (ECS) of (a) OIDA with class-specific-only decay, (b) OIDA with DVED, and (c) a full context baseline. Hypothesis: DVED outperforms class-specific-only decay on retrieval tasks involving mixed velocity domains.
  2. Stage 2: Synthetic Injection of Epistemic Pathogens. Using a synthetic enterprise knowledge graph seeded with known epistemic classes, inject controlled degradation including simulated cross-departmental contradictions to test ZKEC routing layer precision. Measure EIS detection precision and recall for each pathogen category.
  3. Stage 3: Human-in-the-Loop Governance Simulation. Present flagged Knowledge Objects to domain experts and measure agreement rates between EIS alerts and human judgment. This establishes real-world thresholds for alert levels.
  4. Stage 4: A/B Deployment. Deploy the ECG + EIS + ZKEC stack alongside a standard context graph deployment in a partner organization. Measure decision quality delta (accuracy, reversal rate, time to resolution) and epistemic coherence scores between the two conditions.
Section 6

Conclusion and Future Work

This paper has introduced four integrated contributions to the emerging field of epistemic infrastructure for enterprise AI:

  1. DVED: Correcting the uniform decay misspecification by modulating OIDA-style epistemic decay with domain-specific velocity coefficients derived from organizational data.
  2. The EIS: A continuous monitoring layer with a five-category detection taxonomy (Status Creep, Staleness, Ignorance Collapse, Adversarial Anomalies, Epistemic Dark Matter), tiered alert protocol, and immune memory for pathogen signatures.
  3. ZKEC: Our proprietary cryptographic routing mechanism that solves cross-departmental epistemic conflicts via Zero-Knowledge Proofs without violating data sovereignty—a first in the field.
  4. The ECG: Extending Gartner-defined context graphs with epistemic classification, DVED-modulated importance scores, and EIS-maintained health vectors, providing agents not just the history of decisions but their epistemic authority.

Research Agenda: Open questions remain: empirical validation of DVED against organizational corpora with known velocity distributions; calibration of EIS detection thresholds through red-teaming exercises; the exact cryptographic overhead of running zk-SNARKs over graph edges; economic modeling of the ROI of epistemic health monitoring; and extension of the ECG to non-text modalities (audio, video, telemetry).
